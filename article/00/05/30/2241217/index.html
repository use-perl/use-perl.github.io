Perl Whirl 2000, Day 2

pudge on 2000-05-31T16:32:42

Ziggy reporting:

In transit to Juneau. As of 3pm PDT: Next Sunrise: 5:19 am. Next Sunset: 10:15 pm. Weather: Partly Cloudy. Outside Air temp: 50F. Relative Humidity: 80%. Air Pressure 30.1" rising. Sea Conditions: Moderate (4-7.5 ft). Wind: NW, Force 7 (Near Gale). Sea Temperature: 51.8F. Pool Temperature: 77F. Spa Pool Temperature: 76.1.

Today is a half working day. Seminars started at 8:30 this morning. This session was filled with old standbys such as Randal Schwartz's PROM and mjd's ever-popular Tricks of the Wizards, but it also saw some newer material, like Tom Christiansen's application analysis, an intermediate-level introduction to Perl and MySQL by Reuven Lerner, and XML from the ground up, presented by John Simpson.

The non-working half of the day (for spouses and significant others) included a mixer for "geek widows" and "insignificant others" in the morning, as well as the usual compliment of shopping on the Promenade, gambling in the casino, and the ever-popular, never-ending spectacular views of British Columbia.

For the over-75 crowd, activities included a bingo tournament, a wine tasting, an ettiquite class and an art auction (no wonder so many people are flocking to Tom's talk this afternoon!). Tonight is a formal dinner night; prepare for Geeks in Suits and Dresses (and some seriously unique bow-ties to really separate the retirees from the geeks).

Tomorrow, we dock in Juneau, Alaska. On tap for a pub crawl in search of some local flavor, as well as a few thousand other shore excursions involving helicopters, sled dogs, glaciers, kayaks and other such outdoorsy stuff.

Until tomorrow, Z.
