Please Remember

pudge on 2002-09-17T16:36:41

International politics is a lot more complex than it seems. No one says exactly what they mean. Everything they say and do means something else. It's all about putting pressure on other parties, not about the substance of what is said in a press conference or interview.

Right now, it appears that the US and UK have maneuvered Iraq into accepting weapons inspectors (something last week Iraq said would not happen at all; then this weekend said would only happen with certain conditions; then yesterday said could happen unconditionally!). Right now, it appears that the situation got PLAYED by Bush, that there won't be a war (for now), and that the US will get exactly what it wants: its people in Iraq dismantling their weaponry.

All along people have been saying war is inevitable, that Bush just wants war, that this is a vendetta, that Bush is ignoring the international community. And yet, Bush whipped everyone into a frenzy so much that they handed him, in no time at all, exactly what he wanted in the first place! Saudi Arabia has siad they couldl launch any military action against Iraq from their airfields. They "save face" by saying "we didn't change, Bush did." But all Bush did was bring it before the UN, something that was likely going to happen in any event.

Well, it is not over yet, of course. The US is pushing for even more concesssions (although, again, we don't know if they really care about those things or are playing the game of international politics, which they've executed brilliantly thus far). And Iraq has only offered a piece of paper, which isn't very descriptive or binding; the US and UN needs to direct how things are done, not Iraq. But I see little reason not to admire how this has turned out thus far.

And I pray my optimism isn't premature. :-)
