I've also been able to make some connections with siblings or cousins of my grandparents, who I never really knew before. That's really rewarding, in it's own way.

In some ways, it is like work, but I view it more as a hobby, or even being like a detective. There are times that you have to piece together different pieces of evidence, or see what's "most likely" to be true.

For instance, my great grandmother, according to everything I know about her, was born in 1886 in New York City. However, when I requested her birth record, the city said it wasn't in that year. I thought this odd, since I *knew* from talking to my grandmother, that her mother (my great-grandmother) had requested her birth certificate from the city at some point in the past, and had actually received it.

Then, I re-examined some old census records, and in 1900, my great-grandmother is listed as having the birth year of *1885!* I hadn't noticed this before, because it was a write-over of 1886, and the handwriting was messy.

I've since re-requested the record of her birth from NYC, for 1885. I'll be interested to find if she was actually born in that year, because if she was, her parents were married in August of 1885, and she was born in November. If it was November of 1885, then her parents were actually having sex before marriage.

Heaven forbid! ;)

So, while someone may read this, and say, "Who cares about stories of people born over 100 years ago, even if they are relatives," I get great satisfaction from trying to piece these stories together...

Cheers!
