Re:Laws and regulations don't make people good

jmm on 2002-01-18T22:42:42

I really dislike the export controls, but this does demonstrate that they had an effect that can sometimes be construed as positive.

While knowledgeable people could get strong encryption, this case demonstrates that some people are not knowledgeable.

The key issue is: When the person who foolishly chooses 40-bit encryption is a genuine black hat, the export restrictions have proven useful. Of course, whenever it is a white hat who is foolish - they have proven detrimental. I've sure that the tradeoff is greatly to the advantage of not having restrictions, but this is a real example of their benefit (as long as you believe that Al Quaeda files should be subject to examination if possible).
