Re:Zurich on CMS

darobin on 2002-01-05T15:51:47

Yes, most (all?) commercial CMSs are awful beasts that never work. Surprisingly enough, people seem to be aware of that (I guess it's because they get sufficiently in the way of getting one's work done that you can't ignore the fact that they don't work). Most of the CMS buyers face the buy vs build quandary because nothing will fit what they need exactly, but the solutions out there aren't really customizable.

The brutal truths are already out there. I think the main problem is building a sane foundation for a CMS, and that's a difficult problem.

I think Zope got it right when they decided to build a CMF (CM Framework) instead of a CMS. That way you get the basic functionality on top of which you (or others) build apps. It's been a while since I last checked out Zope's progress, and that's what I'm doing these days as I put the final touches to the core bits of my own CMF :)

I'd be delighted to have my brains picked Nat, a certain amount of solipsistic thought about content management has sedimented in there and I could use some inspection of that ;-)

pudge on 2002-01-06T15:34:14

It's certainly web-centric, but it is far beyond being for "a specific kind of website". See my pudge.net, where the primary Slash features are making football picks and viewing personal photos (via the Slash::Picks and Slash::Gallery plugins). It's become a general-purpose applications server, really, with bunches content management features.

Of course, the most polished and included features are geared toward a "specific kind of website," but with the aforementioned plugins, and Autrijus' OurNet plugin (for connecting newsgroups and BBSes to Slash) and a Wiki plugin from chromatic, etc., it is becoming much more.

Yes, it is web-centric. But aside from that ... it's pretty wide-open.
