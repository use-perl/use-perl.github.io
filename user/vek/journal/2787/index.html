jmm on 2002-02-12T14:51:20

I was dining with some friends this weekend and they commented on football teams that are correctly reported on as Irish. Then, when they start to have some success, they start being classified by the media as British teams.

I see the same with Canadian reporting too. If a person was born in Canada, grew up in Canada, did preliminary work in Canada, or achieved success in Canada, they are reported as a Canadian; often without distinguishing which subset of those attributes is being used to claim their achievements as Canadian.

Every community in the world emphasises their own connections to success, while ignoring to some degree the equally valid connections of other communities to that same success.

Eddie was well known partly because the media is always willing to leaven the seriousness of the Olympic movement to a certain degree. Having someone qualify to participate who is not at all close to "the best in the world", and who is entertaining to interview as well is perfect. They get to poke a bit of fun by reporting true events, but the person does come in last so the Olympic committee has their standards maintained.
