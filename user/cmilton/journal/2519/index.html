Unemployment sucks

cmilton on 2002-01-30T04:36:59

After nearly 3 weeks of frenzied contacts with recruiters, a phone interview, and a repeatedly rescheduled phone interview, all my job prospects in Colorado, Kansas, Missouri, and upstate New York have vanished down the drain. I am guessing that the process will begin again next week.

I really feel stupid for leaving my Kansas City job in June 2001 to move back to Colorado. I should have known the layoffs would start happening in Colorado right as I arrived, and that my interviews would end up being cancelled. I should probably never have taken the KC job in the first place.

Been there, done that

ajtaylor on 2002-01-30T07:19:43

I know how you feel. My last paid work day was Sep 20, which included my mandatory 2 weeks pay, so I was technically unemployed longer.

In all that time I've had 3 interviews. I would have gotten the first, but management restricted new hires. The second I didn't have experience in the right area. And the third was an academic position and I just couldn't live on the money it offered.

Keep your chin up. Never stop reading and learning. And don't give up. If you have lots of time, find a project (personal or open source) to contribute to. Keeping busy & productive is essential (speaking from experience).
