pudge on 2003-01-29T14:14:38

If by payroll taxes you include income tax, then perhaps you are right, but usually "payroll taxes" refers to the specific taxes for Medicare and Social Security.

As to the war: he didn't use the words, but the words are "deficit spending." A great many economists believe that a very good way to kickstart a bad economy is to cut taxes and to increase certain spending, which means a budget deficit, which you have to deal with later. This isn't necessarily a bad thing; however, it makes you wonder about why we didn't do more to pay down the debt when we had the chance.

As to dividend tax cuts: yes, stocks are the primary revenue of many seniors -- not just rich ones -- and dividend tax cuts will help them. Of course, helping seniors is not the point of the proposed measure.

As to congressmen: your argument would have more force if you questioned the cutting of congressional staffs and budgets, instead of congresspeople themselves ...

As to health care costs: it is not the only factor, and maybe not the primary one, but it is a major one. And more to the point, it is the one that is specifically driving doctors themselves out of work. It is the reason why doctors left PA, the reason why there is not one trauma center in Las Vegas. The rest of the costs can be passed on to the HMOs and health care plans, but when a doctor faces hundreds of thousands of dollars a year in litigation insurance, he is screwed.

Also, this is the only major factor of rising health care costs that is not currently being addressed, in some way. It's not like the costs of drugs and other expenses are not being worked on (as they have been for a decade now ...).

As to pollution: hey, I'm breathing fine. Screw it.
