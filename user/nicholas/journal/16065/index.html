London.pm had a tech meet last night, at Morgan Stanley's offices in London's Docklands. A couple of people have blogged this already.

The most interesting thing that I gathered there was that Morgan Stanley use several versions of perl in production, not just 5.6.x, but also 5.005 and 5.004. Now, people have been arguing that 5.005 is 4 years old now, surely everyone sane has moved onto newer versions, what's the point of supporting it, ...

I haven't been saying that:

http://archive.develooper.com/advocacy@perl.org/msg00977.html

Yes, I keep harking back to that message, but I consider it important. So does acme, and I'm very pleased that he's agreed to take on the role of 5.005 pumkping (and no, I didn't ask him to, and it wasn't mine to give). Hopefully having 3 separate maintained tracks of perl will provide solid ammunition to counter any FUD merchants who might want to suggest that perl is doing Norwegian Blue impressions.
