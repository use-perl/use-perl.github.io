iBooks, Backlighting and Backups

ziggy on 2002-10-31T21:18:27

I got my iBook on June 30th, and I've been carrying it with me almost every day since. Last week, I went to the Apple store in Clarendon, Virginia, to talk about two problems I've been having. First, the lid doesn't stay closed very much anymore. That's a minor nuisance, but it's getting to be more bothersome.

Second, and more importantly, the backlighting is starting to act funny. On low brightness levels, it's very easy to notice that the backlighting on the left hand side of the screen gradually starts to dim until it's almost unreadable. It starts as a small dim spot towards the middle bottom and grows until it gradually spreads across the entire lefthand side. As a coping behavior, I've noticed that putting the brightness up all the way gradually brings the brightness back on the lefthand side. I tend to use a low backlighting to reduce eyestrain (and preserve battery power), so this is a real problem.

The helpful, friendly Apple store salesperson asked around and said that they might be able to fix the problem with the latch closing, but that the screen brightness was a serious problem, and would require sending the laptop in for servicing. (All Praise AppleCare!) The downside is that there's a possibility that the iBook's hard drive might need to be formatted (but I don't understand why.)

So, rather than getting an iPod at the store that day, I ordered a dual USB/FireWire drive from SmallDog Electronics. (I had narrowed my search down to a LaCie Pocket drive, and they had a good price -- and SmallDog isn't a huge impersonal mailorder shop.)

I ordered the drive with 2-day delivery, and called to complain yesterday that it hadn't showed up yet. FedEx goofed, and they resent the drive, and I received it this morning. Time to start backing up the disk before I send the iBook back to Apple for repairs...
