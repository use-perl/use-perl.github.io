Tiger 'tar' an MakeMaker Don't Mix

cwest on 2005-05-04T02:30:25

Writing Makefile for Email::FolderType::Net MAKE failed! - No such file or directory in   CPANPLUS::Internals::Make::_make at Tue May 3 22:18:56 2005 at   /.../CPANPLUS/Internals/Make.pm line 462

Mac OS X Tiger (10.4) distributes a tar that is metadata-aware. That means that when you use tar to package up your Perl module distribution on Tiger and any of your files have metadata associated with them, that metadata is included in your distribution.

For example, TextWrangler keeps metadata about files. When I edited my Makefile.PL with TextWrangler attributes were stored about my file. When I ran 'make dist' those attributes were added to the archive in the form of '._' files. Including ._Makefile.PL.

That's a problem because, for whatever reason, MakeMaker tries to run ._Makefile.PL and that dies! Therefore my distribution is broken.

I tried to look for ways to shut off this "feature" of the Tiger tar but nothing is obvious. So I build gnu tar from source and stuffed it in /usr/local/bin/tar.

Be careful when packaging your Perl modules on Tiger.
