About fish

jdavidb on 2003-07-08T17:38:54

It's not about letting the big fish eat the little fish; it's actually about letting new little fish come up and compete on the same playing field. An unfortunate side effect is sometimes the big guys win, but that is not always the case. Everything big started out as an underdog. If we didn't have government help and government hindrance, the truly best products and services could actually win.

When we start making laws that specifically acknowledge certain industries and companies, we enshrine forever the state of the art and actually impede progress. We're so busy regulating the old way to do it that there's no way to fit new innovation in under existing laws.

Everywhere I look I see people wanting to treat me differently from a big company. Sometimes that's because they don't want the big company to eat me, but more often than not it's because the big company has special rights that I don't. Journalism isn't something every American can engage in; it's just a special privilege of the media outlets. Recording artistry is the same way. Broadcasting. And on, and on. One might wonder if the big companies don't subtly encourage us to think that these laws "protect" us in order to actually continue to secure government preference.

I know you'll probably disagree, but I see no solution other than to pull the government's hands off and let the chips fall where they may.
