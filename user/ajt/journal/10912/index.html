Re:designers &amp; macs

ajt on 2003-03-05T22:19:23

I think our guy is odd. When he finished his Fine Art degree he started to look at computers and computer design and decided to specialise on PCs as it was a niche skill. Now he is very happy as most customers (he's a contractor) don't like a token Mac on a mostly NT/2K network, very much the case at our place.

We have another contractor, a font designer in at the moment, and he is more Mac literate, but he also said he gets more PC based work than Mac these days. Interestingly he runs MacOS X at home, and rather likes it, and is from what I've gathered, starting to look underneath the pretty skin at the wondrous Unix underpinnings in there.

As I've said before I can't see many people switching from XP to Mac, but it looks very appealing for a lot of Linux users, and NT/Linux hybrid users like myself. You only have to read people's journals round here to see that OS X has a lot of followers that Jobs probably wasn't expecting.
