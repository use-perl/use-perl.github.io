pudge on 2002-04-25T11:53:36

I've not read my Bruce Sterling, but that is the bottom line: laws this bad will be ignored by anyone who can ignore them. There is no possible way that hackers will accept having DRM hardware on their computers, for example. If it must come on every new computer, then people will remove it, or bypass it, or build their own computers. Linux either won't require it or will have a simple hack to get around it. It won't hurt the pirates, who will continue as they always have. It will hurt the media companies -- as many people will now pirate because they CAN, just to show spite -- and the computer companies whose products are now being eschewed. Microsoft is against this because it means a loss of business for them.

I've not heard anything specific, but all other considerations aside, if you want to pirate music and video, and you can do it under Linux easily but not Microsoft, which would you choose?
