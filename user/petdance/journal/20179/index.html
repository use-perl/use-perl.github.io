YAPC::Simple::Chicago

petdance on 2004-08-02T05:27:54

I just floated this idea to Chicago Perl Mongers. There was talk about Chicago hosting YAPC 2005, but I'm not sure we're ready for that. But I think a mini-YAPC might be cool. Not a full-blown YAPC, but some sort of mini-convention, more social than tech.

  • Some weekend in, say, mid-February, far enough away from June/July of YAPC.

  • Weekend = Friday noon to Sunday night.
  • Minimal tech: Maybe one or two talks a day. Lightning talks would be swell. Emphasis is on what makes OSCON so great: The hallway sessions.

  • Family-oriented. People can bring the spouse(s) and kids.
  • Held here in Chicago, or more likely, a suburb like Schaumburg or Arlington Heights, so that people who want to deal with the city can, and those who don't, don't.

  • Costs would be low, only including the actual costs.

  • Announce it far enough in advance that people can plan. Mostly local folks, of course, but who knows who else might show up? "Local" might mean anything from Minneapolis to Detroit to Green Bay to Memphis.
Thoughts? Anyone done something like this?
